# Source Evaluation

Use this reference when synthesizing AI systems engineering, career, and content signals from external sources.

## Evidence Weighting

- Strong evidence: multiple independent sources point to the same demand or pain, especially across hiring posts, engineering blogs, production case studies, active GitHub issues, and credible practitioner talks.
- Medium evidence: a credible company, maintainer, or engineer gives a detailed account, but corroboration is limited.
- Weak evidence: social posts, view counts, star spikes, launch announcements, or a single popular demo without adoption evidence.

## Source-Specific Guidance

- YouTube: Use transcripts and talk substance for claims, patterns, and pain points. Views and thumbnails may reveal attention, not importance.
- GitHub: Check recent commits, releases, issue quality, maintainer responses, contributor diversity, dependency health, and real adoption friction. Stars are an attention signal only.
- Engineering blogs: Prefer posts with architecture details, constraints, failure modes, migration notes, latency/cost data, or production tradeoffs.
- Job descriptions: Separate real requirements from recruiter keyword stuffing. Repeated requirements across companies and regions matter more than one exhaustive posting.
- Community discussion: Treat complaints, workarounds, and repeated confusion as developer-pain signals, but discount unsupported hot takes.

## Market Interpretation

Classify demand as:

- Real engineering demand: teams need people to design, ship, observe, scale, or maintain production systems.
- Buzzword-driven demand: terms appear often but responsibilities are vague or interchangeable.
- Startup demo demand: fast-moving prototype work where speed and integration skill matter more than platform depth.
- Enterprise implementation demand: governance, reliability, security, cost, integration, and change management dominate.
- Research-lab demand: model training, data curation, inference optimization, or research-heavy evaluation work that may require deeper ML background.

## User Fit Filter

Map every recommendation back to this profile:

- Strong Python and backend production experience
- Transitioning toward AI systems engineering and AI infrastructure
- Interested in LLM applications, agents, RAG, evals, observability, data pipelines, and workflow automation
- Values durable career signals, implementation difficulty, and portfolio relevance

Do not recommend deep model research, math-heavy ML specialization, or shallow prompt-only content unless the evidence shows it is strategically useful.
